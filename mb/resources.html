Resources for students

This page contains resources for students. The primary resource is the class community--make a friend or two in class, trade numbers, and don't be afraid to ask for help. Also feel welcome to ask the instructor for help in person before, after, or during class or via email. If helpful, a phone or Google hangout session can also be arranged. Email Greg at greg@missionbit.com.

Beyond these team resources, this class encourages personal research of problems, which is an excellent skill in today's world, but especially applicable to programming. Almost every problem you'll encounter in basic programming (and even advanced programming!) has been solved by someone else, and a quick search on Google should yield an answer. How to "Google" problems is covered in class, and professionals do it constantly, so it's nothing to be ashamed of! For auditory learners, YouTube has an enormous number of tutorials on every subject.
